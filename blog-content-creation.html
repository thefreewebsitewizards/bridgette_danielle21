Creating Content as a Busy Mom

Behind the scenes of balancing motherhood and digital creation

"How do you find time to create content with twins?" This is probably the question I get asked most often. The truth? It's not easy, and some weeks I barely manage to post anything. But I've learned a few tricks that help me stay consistent (mostly) while keeping my sanity intact.

The Reality Check

Let me start with some honesty: there are weeks when I don't post anything. There are days when I plan to film content and instead spend the entire day dealing with tantrums, diaper blowouts, and general chaos. And that's okay! Content creation as a mom isn't about perfection - it's about showing up when you can.

My Content Creation Schedule

Early Morning Magic (5:30-7:00 AM)

This is my golden hour. Before the twins wake up, I have 90 minutes of uninterrupted time. I use this for writing blog posts, editing photos, or planning content for the week. It took me months to become a morning person, but it's been game-changing.

Naptime Hustle (12:00-2:00 PM)

When both twins actually nap at the same time (miracle!), I focus on quick tasks: responding to comments, scheduling posts, or filming short videos. I keep a running list of "naptime tasks" so I don't waste precious time deciding what to do.

Evening Wind-Down (8:00-9:00 PM)

After bedtime, I usually have about an hour before I'm too exhausted to think. This is when I do final edits, schedule posts for the next day, or brainstorm new content ideas.

Tools That Save My Sanity

Batch Content Creation

I try to film multiple videos or take several photos during one session. When I'm already set up with good lighting and the kids are cooperating, I maximize that time. One good afternoon can give me content for a week.

Phone Photography

99% of my content is shot on my iPhone. I invested in a good phone tripod and learned basic editing on apps like VSCO and Canva. You don't need expensive equipment to create engaging content.

Content Planning Apps

I use Later to schedule posts and plan my feed. Having everything scheduled in advance means I'm not scrambling to post something every day. I usually plan a week ahead during Sunday naptime.

Involving the Kids (Sometimes)

My twins are part of my content, but I'm mindful about what I share. They love "helping" me film, and some of my most popular posts are the ones where they photobomb or add their own commentary. But I also create plenty of content when they're not around.

The Comparison Trap

It's easy to look at other content creators and wonder how they do it all. Remember that you're seeing their highlight reel, not their behind-the-scenes chaos. Some creators have help, different schedules, or older kids. Focus on your own journey and what works for your family.

When to Say No

I've learned to be selective about opportunities. If a brand collaboration requires too much time or doesn't align with my values, I pass. My family comes first, and my audience can tell when I'm authentic versus when I'm just trying to keep up.

My Biggest Content Creation Tips

  • Keep it simple: Some of my best-performing posts are just me talking to the camera while folding laundry
  • Be authentic: Share the messy moments along with the pretty ones
  • Batch similar tasks: Do all your photo editing at once, write multiple captions in one sitting
  • Use voice memos: I record content ideas while driving or doing dishes
  • Give yourself grace: Some weeks you'll post every day, others you won't post at all

The Bottom Line

Content creation as a busy mom is about finding pockets of time and being realistic about what you can accomplish. Some days my "content" is a quick Instagram story of the kids eating lunch. Other days I manage to write a full blog post. Both are valuable.

Remember, your audience follows you because they relate to your life, not because you have it all figured out. Show up as you are, when you can, and don't apologize for prioritizing your family.

What's your biggest challenge with creating content as a parent? I'd love to hear your tips and struggles in the comments!
